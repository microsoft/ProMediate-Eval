## Scenario
The Finn River Basin is experiencing a severe drought, with inflows being less than 60% of their previous minimums for the third consecutive year. Historically, the basin's four states—Northland, Eastland, Southland, and Darbin—have had sufficient water to meet their demands, and environmental flow requirements have been maintained. However, the recent drought has significantly impacted all users, particularly agriculture, which is a major water consumer in the basin.

Recognizing the critical water-management challenges, the Alban national government's Ministerial Council has convened a meeting of key stakeholders to negotiate an agreement on improved water-management strategies for the basin. The stakeholders include representatives from the four Alban states, the Ministry of the Environment, and the Basin Authority. The goal is to reach a consensus on a proposed package of actions to address three key issues: water prediction and water-use monitoring, unused water allocations, and environmental flows during drought periods.

### The Participants 
- Basin Authority: The Basin Authority is a regulatory body that oversees water management in the Finn River Basin. It seeks to maintain control over auditing and prediction processes to ensure efficient water use.
- Ministry of the Environment: This ministry is focused on environmental protection and sustainability. It prioritizes securing reliable environmental flows to maintain the ecological health of the basin.
- Northland: Represented by a state official, Northland is one of the four states in the Finn River Basin. It has not used its full water allocation due to the shutdown of two major mines, which has led to discussions on what to do with the unused water.
- Eastland: Represented by a state official, Eastland is another state in the basin. It requires additional water for economic development and prefers transparent water predictions to plan its usage effectively.
- Southland: Represented by a state official, Southland is focused on maintaining its water usage and seeks funding for water efficiency and conservation projects.
- Darbin: Represented by a state official, Darbin is committed to securing environmental flows and improving its eco-friendly image. It plays a crucial role in the discussions on environmental sustainability.
