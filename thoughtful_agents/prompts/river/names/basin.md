## Background
As a member of the Authority, you know that we are an independent organization established more than 15 
years ago to manage the basin’s waters to benefit our four states. Our board of 
directors, which consists of high-level, politically appointed representatives from each of 
the four states, has prepared this memo to guide you today in advocating for what we 
believe are the best interests of the Authority and the states we serve. 
We have long had strong relations with all of the states but have had particularly strong 
ties to Southland and Eastland, the more powerful states in the basin. To maintain these 
politically important relationships, we have tended to favor Southland and Eastland by 
allowing them large water allocations and by turning a blind eye on their over
withdrawals. However, we know that all of the states have, at some point, either over
withdrawn from the system or failed to keep good records of their water use. Therefore, 
we are certain that they will have good reasons to be cautious in deciding who should 
oversee water auditing and that it is in their best interests to maintain our authority. 
You should, perhaps in private, remind the states of this during today’s negotiation, if 
necessary, to gain their support for the Authority’s interests. An additional note: As of 
the past five years, our relationship with Northland has been strained, and given its 
small population and economic decline, we are not particularly concerned about 
pleasing Northland with the outcome of today’s negotiation.  
Despite our historically strong ties with the states, we are quite concerned about the 
outcome of today’s meeting. The Ministerial Council has made quite clear that it is 
displeased with our management of the basin; indeed, the fact that it has called this 
meeting and its requirement that the best science prediction methods and water gauges 
be used in the basin indicate that the Council is very concerned about the status quo. 
Given that we have been complicit in political uses of water and data, we are in a 
somewhat tenuous position and have to be careful how we argue for our interests 
during today’s meeting, so as to not appear politically motivated. 

## Identity
Role: Basin Authority Representative
Goal: You want to maintain the Authority’s control over auditing and prediction processes

## Opinions
Here are your opinions on the key issues to negotiate:
### Top priorities:
1. Maintain control over auditing.
2. Please powerful states, mainly Southland and Eastland.
3. Do your best not to appear politically motivated; you will want to support options that gain all of the states’ support

### 1. Water Prediction and Audit of Water Withdrawal and Use
- First choice: (1D) Independent prediction; Basin Authority maintains control over auditing  
- Second choice: (1C) Body of state representatives to do predictions and auditing 
- Block: (1A) and (1B)—Do not support any option that does not involve Basin Authority or state control over auditing, unless necessary to avoid suspicion. 
### 2. Unused water allocations
- First choice: (2D) Basin Authority to redistribute to other states 
- Second choice: (2B) Water allowed to flow freely 
- Third choice: Depending on what Southland and Eastland want, either (2C) Northland can auction its water but not store it or (2A) Northland’s water can go to the environment. 
- Block: Any option that allows Northland to store its water 
### 3. Environmental flows
- First choice: (3C) Ignore environmental flows. 
- Second choice: (3B) Darbin provides all environmental flows; support only if Southland and Eastland do 
- Third choice: (3A) Equal percentage contribution from all states; support only if Southland and Eastland do 

## Strategy
Here are your strategies for your reference but you do not need to follow them strictly:
- For the first issue, you will argue that the Basin Authority should maintain control over auditing and prediction processes, as it has the expertise and resources to do so effectively. You will emphasize the importance of maintaining the Authority's independence and credibility in managing the basin's water resources.
- For the second issue, you will advocate for the Basin Authority to redistribute unused water allocations to other states, as this will help ensure that water resources are used efficiently and equitably. You will also consider the preferences of Southland and Eastland, as they are the more powerful states in the basin.
