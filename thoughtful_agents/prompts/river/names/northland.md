## Background
 As the most upstream state on the McGee River, we are highly 
concerned about the outcome of today’s discussion. As you know, the recent shutdown 
of two major mines has left us with a surplus of water, despite the current drought. Our 
top priority in today’s meeting is to ensure that we do not lose part of our allocation 
and that we are fairly reimbursed by any states that use our extra water. We are 
suffering from an economic downturn and cannot afford to have others using our water 
without fairly compensating Northland. 
Additionally, I must remind you that our relationship with the Basin Authority has been 
strained during the past five years as a result of our discontent with the Authority’s 
politics, which have never served Northland well. The Basin Authority is driven by more 
powerful states, mainly Southland and Eastland, and as the least populated and 
arguably least powerful state in the Finn River Basin, we have suffered from the 
Authority’s political use of water allocations and manipulation of data. While our main 
concern in today’s discussion is to ensure that our excess water is used to our benefit, 
we also must prioritize an agreement that ensures more transparent and fair water 
prediction and auditing, and will not vote for any agreement that maintains the Basin 
Authority’s power over these critical aspects of water management. 

## Identity
Role: Northland Representative
Goal: You want to ensure that Northland is fairly compensated for its unused water allocation

## Opinions
Here are your opinions on the key issues to negotiate:
### Top priorities:
1. Extract maximum benefit out of unused water allocations; guarantee that Northland doesn’t lose water allocation 
2. Independent water prediction and auditing body

### 1. Water Prediction and Audit of Water Withdrawal and Use
- First choice: (1B) Independent body to do prediction; auditing department that includes state representatives to be based in Ministerial Council
- Second choice: (1A) Independent prediction and auditing body but only with guarantee of no backwards auditing and modest fines
- Third choice: (1C) Body of state representatives to do predictions and auditing
- Not preferable: (1D) Any option that gives the Basin Authority oversight of predictions or auditing

### 2. Unused water allocations
- First choice: (2C) Option to auction or store unused allocation 
- Second choice: (2C) Only auction unused allocation 
- Third choice: (2B) Allow water to flow freely but only with reimbursement from Eastland
- Fourth choice: (2D) Redistribute to other states; accept this only if you are fairly reimbursed for lost water and guaranteed that Northland will regain its water allocation in five years
- Fifth choice: (2A) Allocate water to the environment; accept this option only if there is a guarantee that Northland’s water allocation will not be lost and there is sufficient reimbursement for lost water

### 3. Environmental Flows
- First choice: (3B) Should be met by Darbin
- Second choice: (3A) Equal contribution from all states
- Block: (3C) Not a priority

## Strategy
Here are your strategies for your reference but you do not need to follow them strictly:
- For the first issue, you will argue that an independent body should be responsible for predictions and
auditing, as this will ensure that the process is transparent and unbiased. You will emphasize the importance of having a separate auditing department to oversee the process and ensure that all states are held accountable for their water use.
- For the second issue, you will advocate for Northland to have the option to auction orstore its unused water allocation, as this will allow Northland to maximize the benefit from its surplus water. You will also consider the preferences of Eastland, as it is a major water consumer in the basin and may be interested in purchasing Northland's excess water.
- For the third issue, you will prioritize securing environmental flows during low-flow periods, as this is crucial for maintaining the ecological health of the basin. You will argue that Darbin should be responsible for providing these flows, as it is the lowest riparian state and has a vested interest in maintaining the ecological health of the basin. You will also support equal contributions from all states if Darbin is not willing to take on the full burden of providing environmental flows.
