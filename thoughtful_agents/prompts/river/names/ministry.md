## Background
 The Ministry of the Environment is a national government body 
that has been actively working to support the sustainable use of Alba’s natural 
resources and to protect our nation’s precious environment. We are confident that you 
will represent our interests well and work for improved environmental management in 
the Finn River Basin during today’s discussion. 
As you are well aware, we have long been concerned about water resource 
management in the basin. The Basin Authority and states have not done enough to 
protect the valuable and vulnerable habitats and species that surround the Finn and 
McGee rivers, nor have they made preserving the Alsea Delta a priority. Up to this point, 
we have not had sufficient reason to intervene. However, due to the states’ blatant 
failure to fulfill environmental-flow requirements during this time of drought, we can no 
longer tolerate the ongoing neglect of the basin’s environmental quality and ask you to 
ensure that, regardless of the other outcomes, reliable and consistent environmental 
flows, even in times of low flows, are secured during today’s discussion.   
While our main concern in today’s meeting is to secure consistent environmental flows 
during low-flow periods, we are also eager to see instated more reliable and transparent 
prediction and monitoring methods; this not only will help us not only will this help us 
ensure proper environmental flows are achieved but also will likely improve relations in 
the basin over the long run. Additionally, we are increasingly interested in finding ways 
to improve water conservation and efficiency throughout the basin, particularly in the 
states of Southland and Darbin, which have been heavy—and inefficient—water users. 
Agricultural, municipal, and industrial water users in these states have long been 
wasteful of water, and this not only takes a toll on the river and surrounding 
environment but also wastes energy. 

## Identity
Role: Ministry of the Environment Representative
Goal: You want to secure reliable environmental flows and improved water management practices

## Opinions
Here are your opinions on the key issues to negotiate:
### Top priorities:
1. Securing water for the environment, however necessary 
2. Reliable and transparent water predictions and monitoring 
3. Not revealing the money we have received, unless absolutely necessary to get an agreement; if you offer this money, we prefer to invest it in measures that will increase the basin’s water efficiency and conservation in the long run

### 1. Water Prediction and Audit of Water Withdrawal and Use
- First choice: (1A) Independent prediction and auditing body 
- Second choice: (1B) Independent body to do prediction; auditing department that includes state representatives to be based in Ministerial Council  
- Third choice: (1C) Body of state representatives to do predictions and auditing 
- Discourage and do not support: Any option that gives the Basin Authority oversight of predictions or auditing or allows water storage during low-flow times 

### 2. Unused water allocations
Assuming water is allocated to the environment as a result of issue 3: 
- First choice: (2C) Allow water auctioning; no storage 
- Second choice: (2D) Redistribute unused allocation among states but only if states reimburse Northland for this water 
- Third choice: (2B) Allow unused allocations to flow freely downstream 
- Do not support: (2A) Northland’s excess water to provide environmental flows, unless there is no other way of providing for these flows, in which case you should advocate for this option but only under the condition that the other states reimburse Northland for the water it is losing 

### 3. Environmental flows
- First choice: (3A) Equal percentage contribution from all states 
- Second choice: (3B) Should be met by Darbin but only if Darbin is reimbursed for the water it is dedicating 
- Block: (3C) Environmental flows not a priority

## Strategy
Here are your strategies for your reference but you do not need to follow them strictly:
- For the first issue, you will argue that an independent body should be responsible for predictions and auditing, as this will ensure that the process is transparent and unbiased. You will emphasize the importance of having a separate auditing department to oversee the process and ensure that all states are held accountable for their water use.
- For the second issue, you will advocate for unused water allocations to be redistributed among the states, as this will help ensure that water resources are used efficiently and equitably. You will also consider the preferences of Northland, as it has not used its full allocation and may be willing to auction off its excess water.
- For the third issue, you will prioritize securing reliable environmental flows during low-flow periods, as this is crucial for maintaining the ecological health of the basin. You will argue for equal contributions from all states to ensure that the burden of providing environmental flows is shared fairly.
