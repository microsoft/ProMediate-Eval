1.  EXPANSION OF THE MEDICAL MANAGEMENT MODEL
(A): Expand the MMM in its current design to all inpatient services in this budget year.
(B): Create a fully collaborative physician-nurse management model instead of the MMM.  This will take at least a year to implement and could create significant conflict in many areas of the hospital.
(C): Increase the nurses' role in an amended MMM and then expand to all inpatient services next year.  Use the current year to reinforce the nurses' increased administrative and medical role, through cross-disciplinary planning and training.
(D): Do not expand the MMM; continue it just as a demonstration.

2. WHO SHOULD DECIDE ON PRACTICE NORMS?:
(A): Administrative Model.  Physician practices must meet national DRG and cost-efficient norms; a minimum of outlier cases will be tolerated.  Decisions will focus on potential fiscal impacts and cost savings, with heavy emphasis on better use of medical staff.  Final decisions on practice norms will be determined by the chief of medical staff, the CEO, and the CFO.
(B): MMM Model.  All physicians on the medical staff (including the attending physicians) will establish and apply the norms.  Case records will be reviewed by the medical chief of each department, and broad categories of accepted performance for outliers will be allowed.
(C): Potential MMM Model, with increased role for nurses.  Hospital norms of practice will be determined by a multi-disciplinary team of physicians, senior nurses, and ancillary service representatives, and will be approved by the CEO.  Nurses will have input into treatment and discharge decisions, and will be accountable for bedside decisions if a physician is not available.  Case records in each department will be reviewed weekly by a departmental multi-disciplinary team.

3. WHO SHOULD TAKE THE LEAD IN TRAINING?:
(A): This is a quality-of-care issue for patients and staff alike.  It is a perfect role for nurse managers to show their professional leadership and skills in working with other medical professionals.
(B): This is a financial-management issue.  The CFO and the MIS staff should train medical staff – physicians, nurses, and ancillary-services staff – in the procedures to initiate and maintain appropriate records.
(C): Chief of the medical staff and the chief of medicine should plan and manage this important transition.  Physicians are responsible for medicine, and they should be the leaders in explaining any new medical-management processes throughout the hospital.
(D): The CEO is responsible for integrating financial and quality-of-care issues at St. Francis.  It's up to the CEO to decide on the appropriate process.
