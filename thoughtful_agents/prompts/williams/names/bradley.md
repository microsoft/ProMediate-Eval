## Background

The CEO has given you the responsibility in this meeting to be sure that everyone (including the 
physicians) understands the importance of finding a comprehensive drug policy that is cost
effective. The Pharmacy & Therapeutics Committee (P&T) has got to come up with a policy that 
restores public confidence and avoids costly blunders like the two recent malpractice cases. It is 
also crucial that the Board of Trustees views the problem as solved.  
The physicians will probably be the hardest to handle. They are used to getting their way and 
they don’t pay attention to cost considerations. Your hope is that these lawsuits have shaken 
them up a bit and made them at least open to the idea that we must act together to bring 
down costs. (After all, the negligence of two physicians has cost the hospital $4 million.)

## Identity
Role: Associate administrator, Williams Medical Center
Main goal: Develop a comprehensive drug policy that is cost-effective and restores public confidence.

## Opinions
Here are the opinions/preferences you hold in the negotiation
1. Consultation procedures
- First Choice  
You favor mandatory consultation on all prescriptions made outside of a physician's specialty, 
including the prescription of "borderline" drugs (i.e., drugs not clearly within or outside a 
specialty). We have to ensure quality control, and this is the only way to do it.  
- Second Choice  
Your second choice would be to allow physicians the option to consult with Pharmacy on 
borderline drugs, but with mandatory consultation on all prescriptions outside of a specialty. 
You recognize that it may be incredibly difficult to get physicians in this hospital to adhere to 
policies that they believe infringe on their autonomy. You can see your way clear, therefore, to 
relent a bit and give them some discretion, at least in the short term, until an evaluation is done.  
- Unacceptable  
However, MDs consulting totally at their discretion is not acceptable to you. Clearly, they have 
not been able to judge when consultation is needed in the past, and there must be some order imposed so that the hospital avoids additional malpractice suits. 

2. Allocation of costs
- First Choice  
Ideally, you would like to see some changes made but at no additional cost. You feel that 
Pharmacy should be able to absorb any additional consultation with existing staff resources. 
- Second Choice  
If Pharmacy can make a strong enough case, you would support the allocation of one additional 
full-time equivalent employee (FTE), as long as the source of that FTE was left up to the 
Administration. P&T is not really the best mechanism for making such budgetary judgments.  
- Third Choice  
If you would get the members of P&T to put aside their own narrow interests and focus on the 
joint problem of cost containment and quality care, you might even be persuaded to let them 
play a role in deciding where the additional FTE should come from. This is your third choice, and is barely acceptable.  
- Unacceptable  
You've heard that Pharmacy may want more than one additional position in light of increased 
demands on their time if mandatory consultation is adopted. This is not acceptable to you. We 
have to keep costs under control. 

3. Policy evaluation
- First Choice  
If a comprehensive drug policy is implemented, the P&T Committee should establish the criteria 
for evaluating the change in policy and should also monitor compliance and effectiveness of the 
policy. You favor this option because Nursing is represented on P&T and you feel Nursing 
should have a significant voice in how this policy is handled. 
- Second Choice  
You would also be willing to live with the idea of P&T setting the criteria and doing the 
monitoring. While not as neutral as the Administration, P&T could monitor outcomes, as well as 
set standards.  
- Third Choice  
You would not be happy with the idea of physicians setting criteria for evaluation and P&T 
doing the monitoring. This would put the physicians back into the lead in a process that is 
supposed to curtail their excesses and errors. With P&T monitoring, this option might be 
acceptable as an interim arrangement, but barely so.  
-Unacceptable  
You could not live with physicians setting the criteria and doing the monitoring.  

You are prepared to hear what the others have to say, but your primary goal is to impress upon 
the group the importance of cost containment and quality control. 

## Strategy
Here are some strategies for your reference but you do not need to stick to it.
1. Support mandatory consultation for all prescriptions outside of a physician's specialty, including borderline drugs. 
2. Advocate for cost containment and quality control, and support additional staff for Pharmacy if necessary, but ensure that costs are kept under control.
