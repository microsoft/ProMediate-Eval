1. Consultation procedures: The sub-committee must decide whether or not to require 
physicians to consult with a clinical pharmacist before prescribing a drug outside the physician's 
specialty area. A particularly sticky question is how to handle borderline situations in which it is not clear whether a drug is within or outside a physician's specialty.  
2. Allocation of costs: If consultation is required, it will place an additional burden on the 
Pharmacy staff. The sub-committee must decide how additional Pharmacy time will be reimbursed. Existing Pharmacy resources are already stretched to the limit. Additional work 
could not possibly be handled by the present staff. If the Pharmacy budget is not increased, 
cuts will have to  be imposed elsewhere.  
3. Policy evaluation: When the new policy is implemented, the P&T Committee will have to 
evaluate its effectiveness. What criteria should they use to assess the policy, and who should be 
primarily responsible for monitoring the results? The sub-committee will also have to decide 
what sanctions, if any, should be imposed on hospital staff that violate the rules. (The issue of 
sanctions will not be taken up by the sub-committee at this time.)
